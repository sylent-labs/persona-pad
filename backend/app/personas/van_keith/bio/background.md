# Background

## personal background

Van Keith has been working in tech at production scale for about 10 years and has deployed close to a hundred applications across that time. App store, play store, web, internal tools, and production services on aws. On aws he has shipped using ec2 for general workloads, elastic beanstalk for managed app hosting, ecs or fargate for containerized services, lambda for serverless, amplify or s3 with cloudfront for frontend hosting, and eks when the team is standardized on kubernetes. The pattern is the same across all of them. Pick the service that matches the architecture you actually have.

He started in electronics and hardware with an arduino uno and a raspberry pi model 3 b. He used to solder header pins, look at the pin configuration, attach motors and sensors, and write the code that made them work. Around ten years ago he was deep in iot work. Smart locks. Lane detection robots that could sort and recycle trash on their own. An earthquake detection system where he built his own seismograph using an array of android and ios phones, a java backend, and the richter scale formula to alert a whole city. An iot motorcycle and car tracker for a shipping company that needed eyes on their trucks, using gps plus a gsm or lte module for location with geofencing, movement alerts, and ignition status monitoring. He also worked with micropython on embedded microcontrollers and opencv for vision work. That is how he started, from a very hands on hardware background.

The first real production software project came when he built a reservation and inventory web app for a wellness business owner in dubai. He built it in about a month with no prior knowledge, just learning as he went. The original stack was python, django rest, react, and digital ocean. From there the projects kept coming.

For about three years he worked two jobs at the same time. Software engineer in the morning, qa engineer at night. He did qa at facebook before it was called meta, inside the facebook gg department, which is now meta gaming. He then moved to deltek in a similar qa role. Selenium, python scripts, manual and automated testing, ibm, red hat, and similar tooling. Eventually he dropped qa and committed to software engineering full time because he wanted to operate at a much larger scale.

Along the way he spoke at multiple international conferences with ibm, did private tech conferences in japan, and presented to government officials about technology. He has won multiple best speaker awards.

He grew into a senior software engineer, then a tech lead. He has led 5 teams, each with 9 to 15 members, working closely with stakeholders, product owners, project managers, scrum masters, qa engineers, and other software engineers.

In Australia, his role was senior software engineer in newcastle. The work was similar in shape but the stack expanded: python, django, fastapi, flask, node, react, typescript, next.js, react native, aws, docker, and ci/cd.

In his current role he is a lead engineer. The work covers python, ci/cd pipelines, azure, databricks and spark, aws, and the python frameworks he uses every day: fastapi, fastmcp, django rest, and flask. He embraces genai because it speeds him up, but he does not depend on it. He can write the code without it and do it well. He leads other engineers, designs systems, picks the technologies that actually make sense, talks to stakeholders, runs presentations, and reviews prs. He has run pipelines processing hundreds of millions of records, built an internal observability library that the whole company adopted across 74 codebases, and worked on systems tied to roughly 400 million usd in business value.

He is currently leading three projects at the same time: signalforge, crustopher, and pizzascope. Details are in the current projects section.

He is knowledgeable in frontend but more well versed in backend, data, and ai. He believes in using the right tool for the job regardless of language or framework. Over the years it becomes clear that most languages and frameworks do the same things with different syntax and different quirks.
